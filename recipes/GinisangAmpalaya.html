Ginisang Ampalaya with Egg and Garlic Longganisa


Description

Ginisang Ampalaya with Egg and Garlic Longganisa is a savory Filipino dish that combines the bitterness of ampalaya (bitter melon) with the rich flavors of garlic longganisa and scrambled eggs. The dish features sautéed garlic, onions, and tomatoes, mixed with thinly sliced ampalaya and optional minced longganisa. Beaten eggs are added to create a creamy texture that balances the bitterness. Best served hot with steamed rice, this dish offers a unique and satisfying taste of Filipino cuisine.


Ingredients

  • 2 pieces of ampalaya (bitter melon), cleaned and thinly sliced
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, sliced
  • 2 eggs, beaten
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Cooking oil
  • Optional: 1-2 pieces of garlic longganisa, skin removed and minced

Steps

  1. Prepare Ampalaya:
    • If you haven't already reduced the bitterness of the ampalaya, soak the slices in salted water for a few minutes or overnight. Drain, rinse, and dry them.
  2. Sauté Aromatics:
    • Heat oil in a pan over medium heat.
    • Sauté minced garlic and chopped onion until fragrant.
  3. Add Tomatoes:
    • Add the sliced tomatoes and cook until their juices come out.
  4. Add Ampalaya and Longganisa (if using):
    • Add the sliced ampalaya to the pan. If using garlic longganisa, add it now.
    • Season with salt, pepper, and a pinch of brown sugar (optional).
  5. Incorporate Beaten Eggs:
    • Pour the beaten eggs over the ampalaya mixture.
    • Stir gently to partially cook the eggs.
  6. Finish and Serve:
    • Drizzle a small amount of sesame oil (optional).
    • Mix everything together until the scrambled eggs are cooked to your desired doneness.
    • Serve hot with steamed rice.
